# Tarkovsky — Structural Overview

This section presents a structural, SUBIT‑based analysis of Andrei Tarkovsky’s cinema.  
The goal is not to interpret the films symbolically or psychologically, but to reveal the deep informational architecture that governs their form, rhythm, and metaphysical logic.

Tarkovsky’s films behave as a unified system: a fractal topology of consciousness unfolding across eight octants and sixty‑four states.  
Each film expresses a distinct informational domain, yet all share the same underlying structural grammar.

## 1. Tarkovsky as a Fractal System

Tarkovsky’s cinema is fractal in three senses:

### **1.1 Micro‑scale (shots and textures)**
- slow, continuous camera movement  
- water, fire, wind, reflections  
- suspended time  
- recurring tactile motifs  

Each shot behaves like a micro‑state: a small informational unit containing the essence of the whole.

### **1.2 Meso‑scale (scenes and locations)**
- the Zone (*Stalker*)  
- the dacha (*Mirror*)  
- the House (*The Sacrifice*)  
- the bathhouse and corridors (*Solaris*)  
- the Italian ruins (*Nostalghia*)  

Each location is a self‑contained SUBIT cycle with its own attractors and transitions.

### **1.3 Macro‑scale (full films and anthology)**
- each film corresponds to one SUBIT octant  
- the anthology forms a complete SUBIT‑64 traversal  
- the final film returns to the beginning in transformed form  

This is why Tarkovsky’s cinema feels unified even when the films differ radically in setting or narrative.

## 2. The Eight‑Film Cycle

Tarkovsky’s seven feature films plus his diploma film form a complete SUBIT‑8 cycle:

1. **Origin** — *Ivan’s Childhood*  
2. **Faith** — *Andrei Rublev*  
3. **Memory** — *Mirror*  
4. **Trial** — *Stalker*  
5. **Desire** — *Solaris*  
6. **Exile** — *Nostalghia*  
7. **Sacrifice** — *The Sacrifice*  
8. **Integration / Return** — *The Steamroller and the Violin*  

The cycle ends where it began, but on a higher level of integration.

## 3. Triadic Subject Structures

Many Tarkovsky films operate through triadic subject configurations:

- *Stalker*: Stalker / Writer / Professor  
- *Solaris*: Kelvin / Hari / Ocean  
- *Mirror*: Mother / Father / Narrator  
- *The Sacrifice*: Alexander / Maria / Little Man  

Triads generate interference patterns that produce fractal behavior across scales.

Tarkovsky’s concept of “sculpting in time” is inherently topological:

Time is not linear — it is a field.
